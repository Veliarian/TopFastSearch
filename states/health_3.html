How Air Purifiers Help Those With Asthma

Asthma is a lung condition where your airways in the lungs are swollen and narrow. The muscles around these airways tighten when asthma is triggered, causing symptoms like trouble breathing, chest tightness, coughing, and wheezing.

While there is no cure for asthma, its symptoms can be managed. One such way is to reduce your exposure to allergens in the environment that serve as a trigger to your asthma. The right air purifier might help reduce your exposure to environmental allergens and triggers.

An air purifier is a portable air cleaner that filters and traps pollutants from the air that’s inside your home and sanitizes the air that passes through it. The ionizing air purifier is one of the most popular types of air purifiers, which traps particles using an electric field.

Some of The Common Environmental Triggers of Asthma Inside Your House Are:

– Smoke

– Dust

– Pollen from outside

– Pet dander

– Mold

By trapping these allergens in the filter, an air purifier can take these triggers out of your home. It brings in the air, traps these tiny particles, and then releases the clean air. However, studies have shown inconsistent results as to whether or not air purifiers can help reduce the symptoms of asthma.

One study reveals that air purifiers might be less effective than regular air filters, since they don’t trap particles as effectively as filters, and as a result, these particles might get redistributed to the surface of the purifier. But, another study shows that air purifiers effectively help relieve asthma symptoms, particularly in children.

Overall, how Well an Air Purifier Can Really Help with The Symptoms of Asthma Depends on Certain Factors Like:

– The filter design

– Location of the purifier in your home

– Airflow rate of the purifier

– Size of the allergen particles
