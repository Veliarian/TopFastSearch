Top Personal Loan Lenders for Good Credit Holders

When you have good credit, you have access to a lot of loan options, especially if your credit score is over 700.  You’re likely to be approved for any personal loan you apply for, and you’ll get a good rate as well.

Additionally, the majority of lenders will want to do business with you, but we recommend working with:

Light Stream (Best Overall & Lowest Rate)

Recommended Credit Score: 660+

Minimum/maximum amount you can borrow: $5,000 to $100,000

Truist bank’s online lending arm offers some of the lowest fixed rates around, and there are no fees.

Pros

– Low APRs with autopay

– Wide range of loans available

– Promises to better competitors’ rate 

Cons

– Hard inquiries 

– Longer credit history required

– Due date cannot be changed once repayments start

Payoff (Best for Debt Consolidation)

Recommended Credit Score: 640+

Minimum/maximum amount you can borrow: $5,000 to $40,000

Payoff’s competitive rates, starting at 5.99%, are great for borrowers looking for some breathing room. If you’re looking to consolidate high-interest credit card debt, this is the lender for you.

Pros

– No prepayment penalties

– No late payment fees

– Soft credit inquiry 

– Transparency with respect to loan approval requirements

Cons

– Minimum loan amount is high

– Origination fee up to 5%

– Joint applications are not allowed

SoFi (Best Customer Experience)

Recommended Credit Score: 680+

Minimum/maximum amount you can borrow: $5,000 to $100,000

Along with low rates and a high maximum loan limit, online lender SoFi offers benefits like no origination, prepayment, or late fees. It also gives borrowers access to credentialed financial advisors offering personalized advice.

Pros

– No fees or late/ prepayment penalties

– Exclusive member benefits

– Very competitive APRs

Cons

– Slow funding times

– High minimum loan amount

– High income is necessary for getting the best rates

Discover Personal Loans (Best Minimum Loan Amount)

Recommended Credit Score: 680+

Minimum/maximum amount you can borrow: $2,500 to $35,000

Discover offers competitive interest rates on loans of relatively small amounts – which is perfect if the amount of financing you’re looking for is lower than the minimums set by other lenders.

Pros

– Variety of loan terms on offer, up to 84 months

– Direct payment of creditors (on debt consolidation loans)

– Soft credit inquiry 

Cons

– Low maximum loan amount 

– Restrictions on use of funds

– $39 late payment fee

Marcus by Goldman Sachs (Best for Transparency)

Recommended Credit Score: 660+

Minimum/maximum amount you can borrow: $3,500 to $40,000

Goldman Sachs’ online banking arm offers a comprehensive FAQ section to address all your questions you may have before applying for a loan. They’re remarkably transparent about their fees and rewards.

Pros

– After making 12 consecutive monthly payments (on time and in full), borrowers can choose to defer one payment 

– No late and origination fees or prepayment penalties

– Direct payment of creditors (on debt consolidation loans)

Cons

– No joint applications allowed

– Higher than competitors (generally)

– Low maximum loan amount
