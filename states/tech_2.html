Postpaid V/S Prepaid Plans- What’s Better?

While most prepaid cell phone plans might be cheaper, they usually come with fewer features and perks. On the other hand, postpaid plans might cost more, but they also come with discounts on devices and other benefits including reliable data speeds. But which of the two is actually better? Let’s find out.

In a prepaid phone plan, you need to pay your bill before you receive service. These plans don’t need a contract, so you can pay your bills every month. However, if you don’t pay, your service is cut off.

On the other hand, a postpaid plan allows you to pay your bill after you receive service. Since you aren’t paying upfront, these providers might need a credit check before you sign up for a postpaid plan. 

Pricing

-Unlimited prepaid data plans

Most of these plans are around $40. This includes unlimited data, talk, and text, but offers almost no benefits (like free subscriptions, discounts on devices, or hotspot data). 

-Unlimited postpaid data plans

These plans range between $60 to $70, which is $20-$30 more than the prepaid options. This amounts to $240-$360 every year. 

If you go on price alone, it’s evident that prepaid is a better choice. 

Prepaid v/s postpaid plan performance

-Data throttling

Throttling happens when you experience slow data speeds because you’ve consumed your allotted amount of high-speed data. While all plans have built-in throttling, the data cap differs. Prepaid plans have lower data caps than postpaid plans.

-Deprioritization

Network deprioritization is another way that you can experience slower data speed. This means that your network speed can slow down at any time if the network is busy, like during peak use hours in urban areas, sporting events, or rush hour. While this is an issue with every prepaid plan, it is also an issue with the low tier postpaid ones. If you want reliable and fast data speeds, you’ll have to bump up to the pricier plans.
